Our research program is organized around two complementary directions: computational modeling of neurocognitive variation and translational strategies for personalized interventions.

![Lange Lab Logo](/img/Lab_logo_ry.png)

In computational neuroscience, we develop methods that integrate behavioral and neural data to estimate robust, interpretable biomarkers. These biomarkers are designed to support longitudinal prediction and reproducible analysis across cohorts.

In translational neuroscience, we focus on connecting these computational phenotypes to testable intervention hypotheses. The long-term goal is to build clinically meaningful decision frameworks that are grounded in mechanism and validated across diverse populations.

Current projects include [Multimodal Biomarkers for Cognitive Trajectories](/research/computational-neuroscience/multimodal-biomarkers/) and [Personalized Intervention Mapping](/research/translational-neuroscience/personalized-interventions/).
